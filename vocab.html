Define the Term Question in Past Papers

Rate of reaction: change in concentration of reactant /product with time/ rate of change of concentration

Activation energy: minimum/least/smallest energy needed (by reactants/colliding particles) to react/start/initiate a reaction

Standard Electrode Potential: the voltage obtained when the half-cell is connected to the standard hydrogen electrode; under standard conditions of 298 K and 1 mol dm-3 solutions; electrons flow (in the external circuit) from the half-cell to the hydrogen electrode / the metal in the half-cell is above hydrogen in the ECS / Fe is a better reducing agent than H2/ Fe is oxidised more readily than H2.

Oxidation and reduction: increase in oxidation number and Reduction: decrease in oxidation number

Strong/Weak Acid/Base: (strong) completely dissociated/ionized and (weak) partially dissociated/ionized;

Acid and Base (Lewis theory): electron pair acceptor and donor

Electronegativity: ability of atom/nucleus to attract bonding/shared pair of electrons / attraction of nucleus for bonding/shared pair of electrons

Ligand: species with lone/non-bonding pair (of electrons); which bonds to metal ion (in complex) / which forms dative (covalent)/coordinate bond to metal ion (in complex);

Average Bond Enthalpy: energy needed to break (1 mol of) a bond in the gaseous state/phase; average over similar bonds

Stereoisomers:compounds with the same structural formula and different arrangement in space/3D structures; Accept molecular formula instead of structural formula.

Hybridization: mixing/joining together/combining/merging of (atomic) orbitals to form molecular/new orbitals (of equal energy);

Standard Enthalpy Change of Formation: heat/enthalpy change when 1 mol of a compound/substance is formed; from its elements in their standard states/at 1.01 105 Pa

Standard Enthalpy Change of Reaction: heat transferred/ absorbed/ released/ enthalpy/ potential energy change when 1mol/ molar amounts of reactant(s) react (to form products) under standard conditions / at a pressure 100 kPa/101.3 kPa/1 atm and temperature 298 K/25 Â°C;

Isomers: same molecular formula but differ in arrangement of their atoms
